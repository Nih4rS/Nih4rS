Case Study

American Express Default Prediction

Building an interpretable ML engine that informed lending strategy across millions of cardholders.

Back to portfolio Next project

Overview

American Express provided an anonymized dataset covering 5M+ rows, 189 attributes, and 450K customers. Objective: predict default risk and recommend thresholds that balance growth and loss mitigation.

  • Goal: Deliver a high-precision model with business-ready explainability.
  • Impact: 93.5% AUC and $373M expected revenue lift via smarter default thresholds.

Data Strategy

  • Crafted feature marts spanning spending velocity, delinquency windows, utilization ratios, payment streaks, and macro trends.
  • Applied PCA/VIF checks to mitigate multicollinearity without sacrificing interpretability.
  • Used stratified sampling and cost-sensitive training to mirror business risk tolerance.

Modeling & Evaluation

  • Benchmarked XGBoost, LightGBM, and fully connected neural nets with Bayesian hyper-parameter tuning.
  • Stacked ensembles combined gradient boosting with calibrated probability outputs.
  • Produced SHAP, LIME, and cumulative gains charts to translate model behavior to finance and compliance partners.

Business Enablement

  • Paired risk tiers with interactive simulators quantifying revenue impact at different thresholds.
  • Delivered dashboards summarizing top drivers (spend volatility, payment history, utilization) plus recommended interventions.
  • Documented MLOps considerations covering retrain cadence, drift detection, and governance checkpoints.

Tech Stack

Python, pandas, scikit-learn, XGBoost, TensorFlow, Optuna, SHAP, Power BI.
