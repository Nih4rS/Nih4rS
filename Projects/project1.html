Case Study

American Express Default Prediction

Building an interpretable ML engine that informed lending strategy across millions of cardholders.

Back to portfolio Next project

Overview

American Express provided an anonymized dataset covering 5M+ rows, 189 attributes, and 450K customers. The objective was to predict default risk and recommend threshold strategies that preserve growth while minimizing losses.

  • Goal: Deliver a high-precision model that surfaces actionable drivers and revenue impacts.
  • Role: Lead data scientist overseeing feature design, modeling, validation, and stakeholder storytelling.
  • Impact: 93.5% AUC, $373M projected revenue lift through smarter default thresholds.

Data Strategy

  • Built feature marts covering spending velocity, delinquency windows, utilization ratios, payment streaks, and macrotrends.
  • Applied PCA + VIF checks to combat multicollinearity and reduce noise while maintaining explainability.
  • Balanced the dataset through stratified sampling and cost-sensitive training to reflect business risk appetite.

Modeling & Evaluation

  • Benchmarked XGBoost, LightGBM, and fully connected neural networks with Bayesian hyperparameter tuning.
  • Created stacked ensembles that combined gradient boosting with calibrated probability outputs.
  • Implemented SHAP, LIME, and cumulative gains charts to translate model decisions to finance and compliance partners.

Business Enablement

  • Paired risk tiers with macro-level simulators that quantified revenue impact at different threshold settings.
  • Delivered dashboards summarizing top drivers (spend volatility, payment history, balance ratios) and recommended interventions.
  • Documented MLOps considerations: retraining cadence, drift detection, and governance checkpoints.

Tech Stack

Python, pandas, scikit-learn, XGBoost, TensorFlow, Optuna, SHAP, Power BI.
